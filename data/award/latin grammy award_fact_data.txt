A Latin Grammy Award is an award by The Latin Recording Academy to recognize outstanding achievement in the Latin music industry. The Latin Grammy honors works produced anywhere around the world that were recorded in either Spanish or Portuguese and is awarded in the United States. Submissions of products recorded in regional languages from Latin America and Iberia of Hispanophone or Lusophone countries such as Catalan, Basque, Galician, Valencian, Nahuatl, Guarani, Quechua or Mayan may also be considered.
