Savonlinna (UK: , Finnish: [ˈsɑʋonˈlinnɑ], lit. 'Castle of Savonia'; Swedish: Nyslott, lit. 'New Castle') is a town and a municipality of 33,580 inhabitants in the southeast of Finland, in the heart of the Saimaa lake region.


== History ==
The city was founded in 1639, based on Olavinlinna castle.
